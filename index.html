Ongoing Research

Spatial and Temporal Prediction of Heavy Metal Concentrations in Bangladesh River Sediments (2010–2025) Using Google Earth Engine and Supervised Machine Learning
  • This research aims to forecast 2025 heavy metal concentrations (Pb, Cr, Cd) in Bangladesh’s river sediments by modeling the relationship between land-use changes, climate variability, and laboratory measured pollution data.
  • The study integrates 720 laboratory observations from 48 major rivers with environmental predictors derived from Google Earth Engine, including annual rainfall, urban and industrial land-use percentages, and population density.
  • Random Forest Regression is used to capture complex non-linear environmental relationships. The model follows an 80/20 train-test strategy with 2024 data reserved as a final validation dataset.
  • Expected outcomes include predicted heavy metal concentrations for 2025 and spatial hotspot maps to support environmental policy development and industrial pollution monitoring.

Research Project

ASSESSMENT OF HEAVY METAL POLLUTION IN THE SEDIMENT OF THE RUPSHA RIVER

Objective

  • To determine the concentration of selected heavy metals (As, Cd, Cr, Cu, Pb, Ni, Mn, Zn) in the sediments of the Rupsha River using ICP-MS.
  • To compare heavy metal concentration in the Rupsha River with various rivers around the world from available literature.
  • To assess heavy metal contamination using established indices such as Igeo, Cf, Cd, PLI, Er, and PERI.

Methodology

  • Sample Collection: Surface sediment samples (0–5 cm depth) were collected from upstream, midstream, and downstream stations on July 13, 2024 and combined to create representative composite samples.
  • Sample Preparation: Sediments were oven-dried at 105°C, crushed, and subjected to microwave-assisted acid digestion (USEPA 3051A) using concentrated HNO₃ and HCl.
  • Analytical Analysis: Heavy metal concentrations (As, Cd, Cr, Cu, Pb, Ni, Zn, and Mn) were quantified using ICP-MS and evaluated using multiple ecological risk indices.

Key Findings

  • Manganese and Nickel exhibited the highest concentration levels among the analyzed metals.
  • Nickel concentrations exceeded the Severe Effect Level (75 mg/kg) across all sampling locations.
  • The Igeo and Cf indices identified Cadmium and Nickel as the primary moderate contaminants.
  • The overall Pollution Load Index (PLI) remained below 1, indicating the river is not critically polluted.
  • The Potential Ecological Risk Index (PERI) showed moderate ecological concern primarily due to Cadmium.
